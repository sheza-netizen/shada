📅 The Journey (Survival Story)

📚 Four years of controlled chaos
*left home for a degree… came back a doctor*
Four years away from home — learning medicine, but also learning everything else. The late-night calls, missed family moments, small wins, big setbacks, random trips, and the kind of memories that don’t fit in textbooks. Some days were overwhelming, some days felt like progress, but she kept going through all of it. Not just surviving med school — growing through it, one step at a time 💚

That’s what got her through 💪

A Little Note
April 2025
Dear Shathatha,

I still remember how long you’ve wanted this — how being a doctor was never just a dream, it was always your plan.

From those harsh coaching centre days, the pressure, the constant stress just to get into medical school — to moving away from all of us and building a life in a completely different place… none of it was easy. We saw how much it took from you, even when you didn’t say it out loud.

And then came the years that followed — the exams, the pressure on top of pressure, the ups and downs that never really gave you a break. Late nights, early mornings, and during house surgency, those endless duty hours where sleep barely existed. It was exhausting, mentally and physically, in ways most people won’t ever fully understand.

But even through all of that, you never stopped being you. You were still the one checking in on all of us, making sure we were okay, being there for me and Hidoos no matter how tired you were. You are, without a doubt, the most family-oriented person I know — and that means more than anything 💚

And now… you’ve done it. After everything, you’re finally here — a doctor. 🩺

I genuinely couldn’t be more proud of you. Not even close. There aren’t enough words to explain how proud I am of everything you’ve pushed through, everything you’ve become, and everything you are. This means everything — and you deserve every bit of it.

Even though I can’t be there to watch you walk onto that stage and hear them call you “Doctor,” please know that I’m thinking of you, celebrating you, and feeling that moment just as strongly. And I’ll always be right here — proud of you, standing with you, and cheering you on no matter where life takes you next.

I just want you to be happy in this new chapter you’re stepping into. You’ve earned that, more than anyone.
